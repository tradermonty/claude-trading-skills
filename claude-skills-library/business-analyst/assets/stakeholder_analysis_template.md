## 7. Escalation Path

### 7.1 Escalation Hierarchy

**Level 1:** Project Team
- Business Analyst
- Project Manager
- Response time: 1 business day

**Level 2:** Project Steering Committee
- Business Sponsor
- Department Heads
- Response time: 3 business days

**Level 3:** Executive Leadership
- C-Suite
- Response time: 1 week

### 7.2 Escalation Triggers

Escalate when:
- [ ] Stakeholder conflict cannot be resolved at project level
- [ ] Major scope change requested by stakeholder
- [ ] Stakeholder blocking project progress
- [ ] Resource commitment not being honored
- [ ] Decision required beyond project authority

## 8. Stakeholder Management Best Practices

### 8.1 Do's
✓ Identify stakeholders early and comprehensively
✓ Understand their perspectives and motivations
✓ Communicate proactively and frequently
✓ Involve stakeholders at appropriate times
✓ Build trust through transparency
✓ Document all commitments and decisions
✓ Follow through on all commitments
✓ Acknowledge concerns and address them
✓ Celebrate successes together

### 8.2 Don'ts
✗ Assume you know what stakeholders want
✗ Ignore "difficult" stakeholders
✗ Communicate only when you need something
✗ Over-promise and under-deliver
✗ Surprise stakeholders with bad news
✗ Play stakeholders against each other
✗ Ignore cultural or political dynamics
✗ Use one-size-fits-all communication approach
